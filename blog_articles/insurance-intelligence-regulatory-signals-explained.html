Insurance Intelligence is one of three domains currently in development, built around a category most signal platforms ignore entirely: regulatory change as a leading indicator, not a lagging compliance cost.

Most discussion of insurance and regulation treats regulatory change as something to comply with after the fact. Signal Intelligence's Insurance Intelligence domain treats regulatory signal tracking as exactly what it is structurally: an upstream indicator of risk shifts, pricing pressure, and emerging exposure categories, often visible well before claims data reflects the same change.

Why this domain fits the platform's existing thesis

Regulatory signals share the same fundamental property as every other domain already live on this platform: they move before their downstream consequence becomes obvious. A regulatory consultation document, a draft rule change, an emerging claims pattern in a specific risk category — each of these typically precedes the broader market's pricing and underwriting response by a meaningful window.

This domain draws directly on infrastructure risk adjudication experience — recognising that regulatory and claims-pattern signals behave structurally like every other signal category this platform tracks: quiet, distributed, and informative well before their consequence becomes visible to the broader market.

What the detector stack is designed to include

  • Regulatory consultation and draft rule monitoring across relevant jurisdictions
  • Claims pattern analysis — identifying emerging risk categories before they're broadly recognised as systemic
  • Cross-referencing regulatory signal timing against subsequent industry pricing and underwriting shifts

The same engine, a fourth lens

As with Watches and Property, nothing about Insurance Intelligence requires new MASTER architecture. The same normalisation, CIA scoring, and HPI comparison layers already proven across three live domains absorb this fourth lens using the established integration pattern.
